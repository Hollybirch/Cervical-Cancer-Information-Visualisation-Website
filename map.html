Discussion of Sources

NHS Why People Don't Attend Screening

The information taken from the NHS website is relatable and results could bring comfort to women who also feel the same way about attending cervical cancer screening. Quotes used surrounding the study may offer reassurance to women that are not sure about attending the screening programme. The study did not specify who the women were that were recruited from across the UK, it did not state either the age groups or socio-economic groups used and therefore it is unclear how representative the results are.

WHO Incidence and Mortality Figures

The WHO website offered incidence and mortality figures over time. All data was presented by age-standardised rate (ASR) which made everything comparable. There were vast amounts of data meaning age ranges for incidence and mortality could also be collected. Data was not available for all countries and some countries had certain data unavailable which created some gaps when the data was portrayed over a time frame. The data had to be downloaded individually for each country and then concatenated together to form the whole dataset, although achievable this was a time consuming task. HPV and screening information was also obtained from the WHO website and there was also the limitation of data not being present for certain countries. However, for both the HPV vaccination and screening, the data was mostly available up to 2017. The formatting of the HPV and screening data was not always standardised as yes, no or not available. Some fields had to be altered to ensure that only this information was present for construction of the information visualisation map.

Cancer Research

The data presented by the Cancer Research website was clearly laid out with information graphics that convey the relevant information and also reference where the data has originated from enabling further exploration.

Jos cancer trust risk factors

clearly states associated risk factors with the disease and provides additional information surrounding the topic. Information was found to be quite brief on this website though.
